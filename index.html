How the internet has changed our society

The Internet has turned our existence upside down. It has revolutionized communications, to the extent that it is now our preferred medium of everyday communication. In almost everything we do, we use the Internet.

Here are a few examples of internet applications that have changed our society:

Facebook

One in seven people on Earth used Facebook – 1 billion people, according to founder Mark Zuckerberg. In a decade, the social network has transformed people’s relationships, privacy, their businesses, the news media, helped topple regimes and even changed the meaning of everyday words.

Reddit

Started in 2006, Reddit has become the internet's bulletin board. As a network of communities, Reddit's core content consists of discussions organized into user-created areas of interest called "subreddits". There are about 138,000 active subreddits among a total of 1.2 million, as of July 2018. By allowing users to join their cluster of niche-interests, reddit connects together unseen facets of the world and give them the attention they deserve.

Wikipedia

Wikipedia was launched on January 15, 2001, by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger. Sanger coined its name, as a portmanteau of "wiki" (the Hawaiian word for "quick") and "encyclopedia". While it was Initially an English-language encyclopedia, versions in other languages were quickly developed. With at least 5,969,381 articles, the English Wikipedia is the largest of the more than 290 Wikipedia encyclopedias. Overall, Wikipedia comprises more than 40 million articles in 301 different languages and by February 2014 it had reached 18 billion page views and nearly 500 million unique visitors per month.

Made by Zak Benhalima

YouTube

YouTube allows users to upload, view, rate, share and comment on video files. It offers a wide variety of user-generated and corporate media videos.

Amongst others, available content includes video clips, TV show clips, music videos, short and documentary films, audio recordings, movie trailers, live streams, and other content such as video blogging, short original videos, and educational videos.

As the largest video hosting website and the second largest search engine in the world, YouTube continues to have a tremendous impact on many areas of life, including society.
